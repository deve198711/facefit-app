Is Expensive Sunscreen Really Better?

Price doesn’t always equal performance — especially in skincare. So what’s the real difference between that $8 drugstore sunscreen and the $60 luxury brand? Let’s find out.

1. What Makes Some Sunscreens Expensive?

  • ✨ Premium ingredients like antioxidants, peptides, or botanical extracts
  • 🧴 Elegant textures that feel more like moisturizer than sunscreen
  • 🧪 Patented UV filters that offer better wear or protection
  • 🌿 Fragrance-free, reef-safe, vegan, or hypoallergenic certifications

High-end sunscreens may feel better and include skin-benefiting extras, but they don’t always offer better UV protection than cheaper ones. That depends on the SPF and PA ratings — not the price tag.

2. SPF 30 vs. SPF 50 — What Should You Choose?

It’s not about which one is better — it’s about what fits your lifestyle:

  • 🏢 Indoor or casual use: SPF 30 is usually enough for most people who stay indoors.
  • 🏖️ Outdoor or active life: Go for SPF 50+ — you’ll need higher protection from stronger sun exposure.

3. Do You Reapply Sunscreen Often Enough?

Dermatologists recommend reapplying every 2–3 hours, especially if you’re sweating or in direct sunlight. The truth? Most people don’t apply enough in the first place.

  • 🧴 A nickel-sized amount for your face
  • 🧴 A shot glass amount (about 1 ounce or 30ml) for your entire body

Source: American Academy of Dermatology

4. Final Verdict

You don’t need to break the bank to protect your skin. Affordable sunscreens can work just as well — if they have the right SPF and you apply them properly. Expensive ones may feel nicer or offer bonus ingredients, but they’re not always “better.”

Choose sunscreen based on your skin type, comfort, and how often you're realistically going to use it — and don’t forget to reapply!
